About UW2C

The primary aim of UW2C, short for "Underwater Cleaner and Connector," is to revolutionize the world of underwater technology with innovative solutions that address two critical challenges. First and foremost, UW2C seeks to protect and ensure the integrity of underwater cables, which are essential for global communication. By developing an autonomous two-wheeler robot equipped with advanced sensor technology, UW2C aims to create a protective barrier around these underwater optical cables. The robot continuously monitors the distance between itself and the cables, promptly detecting and reporting any potential damage, such as from shark bites or ship anchors. This immediate response capability aims to significantly reduce downtime and data loss, ensuring uninterrupted global communication. Secondly, UW2C is committed to combating water pollution in aquatic environments. To achieve this, they have designed an innovative boat robot powered by sustainable DC motors and Arduino technology. This robot is dedicated to efficiently collecting debris, plastics, and other pollutants from water bodies, thereby contributing to the preservation of marine life, biodiversity, and overall ecosystem health. UW2C's overarching aim is to harness innovation and technology to make a positive impact on the environment and global communication infrastructure, all while championing a more sustainable and connected world beneath the surface of our waters.

Project Report

Project Report And Challanges.

**Project Report: UW2C - Underwater Cleaner and Connector** UW2C, the "Underwater Cleaner and Connector" project, was initiated to tackle two pivotal challenges in underwater technology. With a focus on "Connecting the World Underwater" within the World Robotics Olympiad (WRO), UW2C aimed to safeguard underwater cables and combat water pollution. Achievements include the development of an autonomous cable protection robot and an efficient pollution-collecting boat robot, both showcasing versatility and autonomy. Challenges involved adverse underwater conditions, power supply reliability, sensor integration, pollution identification, and long-term durability. Despite hurdles, UW2C remains dedicated to advancing its solutions, contributing to a sustainable and interconnected underwater world. This project exemplifies the transformative potential of technology in addressing global challenges.
